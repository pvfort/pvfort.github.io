My Journey

I started my journey in astronomy because I was fascinated by how much we can learn from just the light of distant stars. Currently, I am a Master's student working on tracing Star Formation Rates using Hα emitters.

My thesis focuses on the LAGER fields. Using Python tools like astropy and photutils, I analyze large datasets to find galaxies that existed billions of years ago. It involves a lot of coding, data reduction, and drinking coffee!

Beyond Astronomy

When I am not debugging code or writing papers, I love exploring the outdoors. Living in Chile provides amazing opportunities for hiking in the Andes.

I am also passionate about science communication and making astronomy accessible to everyone. You can check out my outreach page to see some of the workshops I've organized.
