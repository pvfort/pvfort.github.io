I'm 25 years old and living in Santiago. I currently serve as the Master's representative at the Institute of Astrophysics.

What I'm interested in

I study Galaxy Formation and Evolution and High-z Galaxies. I'm working to figure out the evolution of the Star Formation Rate Density (SFRD) across cosmic time. To do this, I use Emission Line Galaxies from the LAGER survey, where I'm an active collaborator.

My favorite part of astronomy is going to observatories and getting my hands on real data!

When I'm not observing, I spend a lot of time wrestling with data, running LePHARE, and making plots look pretty in Matplotlib.

Building a Community

Science is better together! I organize the Python Meetings at my institute. It's a relaxed space where we share coding tips, complain about bugs, and drink coffee.

Beyond that, I'm on a mission to bring people together. After our institute suffered a fire a few years ago, many student traditions were lost. My goal was to create a safe space for everyone to join on Fridays! We call them PIE Fridays, and they have helped shape a beautiful community at IA!

Beyond Science

When I'm not looking up at distant galaxies, you can find me baking or gardening. I really enjoy having quiet time to recharge.
