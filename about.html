My Journey

I am an M.Sc. student in Astrophysics at the Pontificia Universidad Cat√≥lica de Chile (PUC), where I also completed my Bachelor's degree. My passion lies in understanding how galaxies formed and evolved in the early universe.

My research specializes in emission-line galaxies. I use data from the LAGER survey (Lyman Alpha Galaxies in the Epoch of Reionization) and instruments like DECam to measure star formation rates at redshifts $z=0.47$, $0.93$, and $1.59$.

Technical Expertise

I have extensive experience with Python (Astropy, NumPy, Pandas) and astronomical software like SExtractor, Topcat, and CIGALE. I also enjoy the observational side of astronomy, having observed with the Magellan Baade Telescope and the NTT at La Silla.

Community & Leadership

Beyond research, I am deeply involved in the academic community. I organize the Institute of Astrophysics Python Meetings, creating a space for students and postdocs to share coding skills. I also serve as the Master's Representative, helping to bridge the gap between students and the faculty.
